Employees must have permission from the President to use any company tools for personal

use. Field employees must have the hand tools that are always required of their trade with them during work.

All field employees are expected to have the following personal tools available to them while at work as a condition of their employment:

□ Hammer □ Framing Square □ Small sledgehammer □ Plumb bob □ Tin snips □ Cats paw/pry bar □ Tape measure 25’ □ Crescent wrench □ Tape measure 100’ □ 5 in 1 screwdriver □ Dry line □ Handsaw □ Chalk line □ Drill bit index □ Nail bags □ Nail sets □ Speed Square □ Utility knife □ Pencils □ Wood Chisel □ Torpedo level □ Linesman pliers □ 2’ level □ Hacksaw □ 4’ level □ Sliding T bevel

All field employees shall have available to them on any job site the tools found on the MHC

employee tool list. This is a minimal set of hand tools that any carpenter should have.

Any employee not already possessing these tools should make provisions within 2 months

to acquire them. If this creates a financial burden, then MHC will make a short-term loan to the employee with paycheck withholding to assist the employee in acquiring these tools within the time provided.

Return of Company Property & Final Paycheck

Employees are required to return all company property upon separation of employment.

Final paychecks may be withheld only as permitted by applicable law until all company

property has been returned. Where allowed by law and with any required written authorization, the company may deduct or recover the value of unreturned or damaged property from an employee’s final wages.
