Code of Conduct

MH Construction, Inc. requires all team members to comply with all company policies and directives. Team members are to perform their duties in a cooperative and efficient manner, demonstrating high regard for work habits, attitude, production, and personal conduct. We expect team members to refrain from discussing contentious or personal topics involving other team members and salary amounts, and to conduct themselves professionally at all times.

Whether you are a current or former team member of MH Construction, Inc., under no circumstances may you use, transfer, or convey by any means—including e-mail—equipment, purchased software, computer software developed by yourself or another team member, Client Partner lists, or any other company-owned materials or intellectual property belonging to MH Construction, Inc., or use such for other employment or for the benefit of any other company or person outside the company, without the express written permission of the President. Personal use of company property may be authorized for the benefit of a team member under special circumstances.

Team members should leave valuable personal items at home. MH Construction, Inc. cannot be responsible for the loss, theft, or damage of any personal property brought to the office. Management works hard to accurately analyze the value of various job tasks, educational requirements, and experience; your proficiency and that job's value to the company determine your pay. Anyone accessing payroll records without specific authorization will be subject to immediate termination. Gossip destroys team morale, and management will not tolerate negative discussions among team members about management or other team members. If you have a question about an event or a policy, go directly to your supervisor and ask for clarification.

Dress Code

MH Construction, Inc. expects that each team member's appearance will be consistent with a professional image. All team members are expected to dress in attire appropriate to their position and duties. Clothing should be neatly laundered, and all team members must practice good hygiene. For field team members, no earrings or other jewelry that may be considered a safety concern by the immediate supervisor may be worn during work hours, and any safety gear required by the job superintendent must be worn on site. Office team members should wear appropriate, non-revealing clothing that would be considered professional by most standards.

Drug and Alcohol Use

MH Construction, Inc. team members are expected to report for duty in a manner that provides for public safety and efficient performance. The possession, use, distribution, manufacture, or sale of alcohol and/or controlled substances is prohibited. A team member is prohibited from having alcohol and/or controlled substances in a company or personal vehicle on a jobsite, and may not work while under the influence of alcohol and/or a controlled substance. Prohibited drug use may include over-the-counter medication when it is used in a manner inconsistent with its intended use and/or affects the performance of the team member. The use of alcoholic beverages during working hours or lunch breaks is strictly prohibited; no alcoholic beverages are allowed on any MH Construction, Inc. job site or company property, including office locations and warehouses.

As a condition of continued employment, team members may be required to undergo random alcohol and drug screening. A screening may also be required when a team member is party to a workplace injury that results in damage to themselves, a fellow team member, or company property. Failure to take part in a drug or alcohol screening may be cause for termination. Team members violating this policy may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Non-Smoking

MH Construction, Inc. endeavors to provide a safe and healthy working environment for all team members and therefore prohibits smoking in any location other than designated areas, including company buildings, job sites, and company vehicles. Team members who violate this policy may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.

Gifts and Tips

Team members are not to accept gifts, tips, gratuities, or special favors from Client Partners or vendors, and are prohibited from soliciting money, gifts, or special favors from other team members or their families. MH Construction, Inc. realizes that there may be certain solicitations, such as school fundraisers, that team members would like to make available to their colleagues; contact your supervisor before bringing any solicitation to your workplace.

Company Property

If you leave your employment with MH Construction, Inc., under no circumstances may you use, remove, or fail to return any company equipment, tools, purchased software, software developed by yourself or another team member, Client Partner lists, or any other company-owned materials or intellectual property, without the express written permission of the President. Final checks will not be distributed until all company property is accounted for, or until management has agreed to release the final paycheck.

Confidentiality

MH Construction, Inc. requires all team members to sign a confidentiality and non-disclosure agreement as a condition of employment, due to the possibility of being privy to information that is confidential and/or intended for the company's use only. This policy protects the interests of MH Construction, Inc. by safeguarding confidential, unique, and valuable information from competitors or others. Should an occasion arise in which you are unsure of your obligations, it is your responsibility to consult with the President of the company. Failure to comply could result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
