Time Keeping

The Federal Government requires that MH Construction, Inc. maintain accurate time tracking for hourly team members. All team members are expected to keep track of their hours worked, and any unauthorized team member who alters the report of another team member's time worked will be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination. MH Construction, Inc. utilizes HH2 software for generating timesheets; a username and password will be given to each new team member on their first day of employment. Team members should not rely on their supervisors to enter their time in HH2. HH2 time must be entered daily and signed as complete and accurate on Fridays by end of day, and time will be approved by supervisors Monday morning, after the last day of the pay period. Failure to turn in time sheets will result in progressive disciplinary action, which may include a verbal warning, written warning, disciplinary suspension, or termination of employment.

Regular Pay

Team member benefits are a significant part of your total compensation. Paychecks are distributed weekly, five business days after the end of the weekly pay period. When the distribution day falls on a holiday, paychecks will be distributed on the preceding business day.

Overtime

Overtime is defined as time worked beyond a forty-hour period from Monday through Sunday. Hourly-waged team members will be compensated on a time-and-a-half basis for overtime, and team members are to complete their daily and weekly assignments during regular office hours. Non-exempt team members are eligible for overtime for all hours worked that exceed 40 in any work week. It is expected that a team member will comply with any reasonable request to work overtime, and MH Construction, Inc. will try to give you as much notice as possible when overtime is necessary. All overtime designated by your manager is approved overtime; if a team member works overtime without approval, the overtime must be paid, but the team member may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination. Exempt team members will not be paid on an overtime basis and may be asked to work extra hours as business demands or as otherwise necessary.

Bonus and Incentive Pay

Bonus or incentive pay is additional compensation paid to a team member, or group of team members, in addition to their normal rate of pay. This additional compensation may be granted under specific, limited circumstances.

Garnishment of Wages

MH Construction, Inc. honors wage garnishments. Team members will be notified immediately when garnishment orders are received from the courts or other governing agencies.

Benefits Eligibility

Team members shall work a minimum 30-hour work week to be entitled to benefits. Willfully making a false statement for the purpose of obtaining or denying benefits may result in criminal prosecution and termination of employment.

Medical Insurance

MH Construction, Inc. offers medical insurance to all full-time team members. Details of the plans may be found in the Benefit Booklet; this handbook does not constitute such a legal document. The company offers medical coverage for eligible team members and their eligible dependents, administered by a major medical insurance carrier or health maintenance organization (HMO). A team member's contribution for coverage will be deducted from salary based on benefit selections. Your Summary Plan Description (SPD) contains more details; in the event of any conflict between this handbook and the company's SPDs, the SPDs shall govern. These plans are subject to change at the company's discretion, as is the amount you may be required to contribute toward the premiums. Full-time team members are eligible to participate after fulfilling a 60-day probation period, with benefits beginning the first of the following month.

Team Member Contributions

The company's benefit package is contributory; you are responsible for a portion (25%) of the premium for your benefits. If you choose insurance coverage for more than just yourself under the plan, you will be responsible for 100% of the additional premium. A portion of the premium, up to a maximum per month, is contributed by the company, and your contributory cost is deducted from your paycheck.

Enrollment

At the time you are hired, you are given an opportunity to elect certain benefits. If you waive participation for yourself or your eligible dependents, you will generally be allowed to apply only during Open Enrollment. The Open Enrollment period, determined by the insurance company, allows team members to add or change coverage; changes take effect on the effective date following the period, and once made, a selection cannot change until the next Open Enrollment except in the case of a major life status change. A qualifying event—such as marriage, birth or adoption of a child, or involuntary loss of medical and/or dental coverage—may allow Special Enrollment as long as application is made within 30 days of the event. For specific details, refer to your Summary Plan Description.

Continuation of Health Coverage

Federal law generally requires employers with 20 or more employees to give team members, spouses, and dependent children the right to continue group health benefits for limited periods under certain circumstances, such as voluntary or some involuntary job loss, reduction in hours worked, death, divorce, and other life events. Team members ordinarily may continue their health coverage for up to 18 months when their employment ends, subject to the specific terms of the plan and applicable law.

Washington Paid Family & Medical Leave and WA Cares

All team members have premiums withheld from their paycheck to contribute to the Washington Paid Family and Medical Leave fund as well as the WA Cares fund. These are state-run short-term coverage programs, and you must file with the state upon qualifying.

Workers' Compensation

To provide for payment of your medical expenses and partial salary continuation in the event of a work-related accident or illness, you are covered by workers' compensation insurance, provided by the company and based on state regulations. The amount of benefits payable, and the duration of payments, depends on the nature of your injury or illness; all medical expenses incurred in connection with an on-the-job injury or illness and partial salary payments are paid in accordance with applicable state law. If you are injured or become ill on the job, you must immediately report it to your manager and the Human Resources Department so the company can help you obtain appropriate medical treatment. Failure to follow this procedure may delay or jeopardize your benefits.

Social Security and Medicare

Through Social Security, you and your family are protected against loss of work income due to retirement, disability, or death. Medicare protects you against the increasing medical expenses associated with aging. All team members participate in the Social Security program, and you and MH Construction, Inc. both contribute on your behalf.

Unemployment Compensation

The U.S. Department of Labor's unemployment insurance programs provide unemployment benefits to eligible workers who become unemployed through no fault of their own and meet certain other eligibility requirements. Unemployment insurance is a joint state-federal program that provides cash benefits to eligible workers; each state administers a separate program under the same guidelines established by federal law. This plan is financed by employers and a small portion by the team member, through payroll taxes.

401(k) Retirement Plan

The 401(k) plan is a workplace retirement plan that allows team members to defer a certain portion of their wages into a retirement account, which they can then invest for the future, receiving certain tax advantages in both the short and long term. Team members become eligible to contribute to the 401(k) plan after 12 months of employment and may contribute between 1% and 90% of their eligible compensation, before or after tax as permitted by the plan.
