A Culture of Respect

It is the policy of MH Construction, Inc. to foster an environment of respect for the dignity and worth of all team members. Strong working relationships, grounded in mutual respect and understanding, are the foundation of our success. Every team member is responsible for helping to maintain a workplace that is professional, inclusive, and free of harassment and discrimination.

Equal Employment Opportunity

MH Construction, Inc. provides Equal Employment Opportunity to all team members and applicants for employment in accordance with all applicable Equal Employment and Affirmative Action laws, directives, and regulations of Federal, State, and Local governing bodies or agencies. MH Construction, Inc. will not discriminate against or harass any team member or applicant on the basis of race, color, creed, religion, national origin, sex, sexual orientation, disability, age, marital status, or status with regard to public assistance. We take affirmative action to ensure that all practices are free of such discrimination, including hiring, upgrading, demotion, transfer, recruitment or recruitment advertising, selection, layoff, disciplinary action, termination, rates of pay or other forms of compensation, and selection for training.

MH Construction, Inc. supports the policies of the Americans with Disabilities Act and is committed to treating all applicants and team members with disabilities in accordance with the requirements of that act.

Anti-Harassment and Discrimination Policy

Because incidents of harassment are demeaning to everyone involved and impair the company's ability to perform, MH Construction, Inc. will not tolerate any conduct that sexually, physically, or emotionally harasses another team member by intimidation, retaliation, verbal abuse, or in any other way that demeans or discriminates against any team member.

Harassing behavior has the effect of creating an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment and is prohibited in the workplace, whether committed by supervisory or non-supervisory personnel. Harassing conduct includes, but is not limited to, unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and all other verbal or physical conduct of an offensive sexual nature. It may also include offensive and derogatory comments, jokes, e-mails, innuendoes, graphic verbal comments about an individual's body, degrading words used to describe an individual, or displaying or possessing sexually suggestive objects or pictures in the workplace.

Reporting and Non-Retaliation

Any team member who believes he or she has been harassed should immediately notify his or her supervisor or the President. All complaints and related information will be investigated and kept confidential to the extent possible without compromising an investigation. MH Construction, Inc. prohibits retaliation against any team member who, in good faith, reports harassment or discrimination or participates in an investigation. Retaliation is itself a violation of this policy and will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
