Personal Information

It is important that the personnel records of MH Construction, Inc. be accurate at all times. All team members are required to inform the company of any change in name, address, phone or cell number, person to be notified in case of an emergency, email address, or marital status and dependents. MH Construction, Inc. does not give out personal team member information.

Employment Categories

  • Probationary. All newly hired team members are considered probationary until completing 90 days of employment and receiving a satisfactory evaluation from management.
  • Part-Time. A part-time team member works less than 30 hours per week; hours must be approved on a weekly basis if they exceed 30 hours. Part-time team members are paid hourly, may not be eligible for company benefits, are eligible to accrue paid time off, and must accurately track daily time worked by weekly timecards.
  • Temporary. A temporary team member is retained on a limited, as-needed basis for special projects, may not be eligible for company benefits, and must accurately track daily time worked by weekly timecards.
  • Non-Exempt (Hourly). A non-exempt team member works more than 30 hours per week, is paid hourly, and is expected to maintain an in-and-out time record by weekly timecards. These positions are eligible for overtime pay if more than 40 hours are worked in a workweek. Any overtime or nonstandard hours must be approved, before they occur, by the job superintendent or management.
  • Exempt (Salaried). These positions are excluded from the overtime provisions of the Fair Labor Standards Act. An exempt team member is paid a salary regardless of the number of hours it takes to complete weekly tasks and is eligible for the same benefits as full-time team members, apart from overtime compensation or compensation time. Salary takes into consideration skill level as well as the hours the job demands.

A team member hired by MH Construction, Inc. will be on probation for the first 90 days, set up by the supervisor at the time of hire. The probationary period is the final phase of the selection process, used to ensure the new team member possesses the technical and interpersonal skills needed to perform the job satisfactorily and to interact successfully with the MH Construction, Inc. team. Failure to receive a satisfactory evaluation during or at the end of the probationary period will result in termination.

Work Schedules

The normal MH Construction, Inc. work week is Monday through Friday. Operating hours for field team members are between 7:00 AM and 3:30 PM. Normal office hours are from 7:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Every full-time team member is expected to work at least an eight-hour day, five days a week, though a supervisor and team member may agree to modify this schedule with management approval. Field team members are expected to arrive at their assigned job sites—not the office—ready to work at the start of the day, and office team members are expected to arrive at the office at the start of the day.

Full-time team members are allowed a 30-minute lunch period, and part-time team members' lunches are scheduled as needed. All breaks offered will be in accordance with state laws, and if the company requires you to work through your lunch period, the company will pay for your lunch period. If you are going to be late, you must contact your supervisor as well as the General Manager; failure to do so will result in a verbal warning, after which your employment status will be under review and subject to termination.

Attendance

Chronic, habitual, or excessive lateness or absenteeism will not be tolerated. Lateness, absenteeism, and failure to follow call-in procedures are causes for disciplinary action up to and including termination. MH Construction, Inc. has some very busy months, usually March through September, during which team members are required to be available for longer, busier days; we discourage vacations during these months but will do our best to accommodate requests. When reporting sick, report to the General Manager as well as the superintendent you are assigned to that day; if you are a superintendent or working on a site by yourself, report to the General Manager, Manpower Coordinator, or the President.

Performance Reviews

Your performance will be reviewed during and at the end of your 90-day probationary period, as well as yearly throughout your employment. If your performance is found satisfactory following the probationary period, you may become a fully participating staff member eligible for group benefits, as outlined in the employment categories. All team members should know how they are performing and what steps they might take to improve. At the time of review, you will meet with your immediate supervisor to discuss strengths and weaknesses, career development, goals, and objectives. A written review will be placed in your personnel file after that discussion, and you will be asked to sign it to acknowledge the conversation; a copy will also be given to you for your personal files. Pay increases are not part of the review process unless indicated by your supervisor, and all pay increases are based on merit.

Outside Employment

If, while employed with MH Construction, Inc., a team member becomes involved in other employment, it is essential that their primary commitment is given to MH Construction, Inc. This commitment includes honoring your work schedule at MH Construction, Inc. While employed here, a team member may not use talents or knowledge gained through employment at MH Construction, Inc., company equipment, software, or other company assets for the benefit of another company or non-MH Construction, Inc. staff, without the express permission of the President.
