Our Approach to Performance and Conduct

MH Construction, Inc. expects every team member to conduct themselves in accordance with generally acceptable work behaviors and to uphold the standards described throughout this handbook. When concerns arise, our goal is to address them fairly, clearly, and—wherever possible—in a way that helps the team member succeed.

Performance Improvement

When performance concerns arise, a team member may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) for a period to be determined by their supervisor. A PIP is intended to clearly define expectations and give the team member a defined opportunity to meet them.

Disciplinary Process

Team members are expected to conduct themselves in accordance with generally acceptable work behaviors. Disciplinary action will depend on the circumstances surrounding the violation and will include termination if necessary. Where appropriate, the company may use progressive discipline—such as a verbal warning, written warning, disciplinary suspension, or termination—but MH Construction, Inc. reserves the right to determine the appropriate level of discipline based on the situation. Your employment is not guaranteed for any specified length of time, and MH Construction, Inc. reserves the right to terminate team members with or without cause.

Termination and Resignation

Your employment is not guaranteed for any specified length of time. You have the right to terminate your employment with or without cause, and MH Construction, Inc. reserves the right to terminate team members with or without cause. If you decide to voluntarily leave MH Construction, Inc., you are expected to provide two weeks' notice before terminating your employment; if you do not give this notice, your supervisor may require immediate departure. Any keys or other property of MH Construction, Inc. must be turned in upon termination, and if company property is not returned before employment is terminated, the estimated value of the property may be deducted from the team member's final check.

After employment at MH Construction, Inc., former team members may not use proprietary knowledge, software, software routines, Client Partner lists, or other company assets for the benefit of other companies or non-MH Construction, Inc. staff. When you leave MH Construction, Inc., you may be asked to participate in an exit interview, which gathers a variety of information and assists the company in addressing team member concerns and explaining various benefits that may extend beyond the date of separation.

Return of Company Property and Final Paycheck

Team members are required to return all company property upon separation of employment. Final paychecks may be withheld only as permitted by applicable law until all company property has been returned. Where allowed by law and with any required written authorization, the company may deduct or recover the value of unreturned or damaged property from a team member's final wages.
