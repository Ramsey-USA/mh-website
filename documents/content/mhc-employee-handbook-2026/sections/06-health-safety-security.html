Our Safety Commitment

Safety is always a top priority at MH Construction, Inc. Safe work practices and good housekeeping habits are responsibilities each of us shares. We provide our Client Partners and ourselves with a safer environment by keeping areas clear and hazard-free and not taking unnecessary risks. Every team member has both the authority and the obligation to stop work when a condition is unsafe.

OSHA Compliance and Job Hazard Analysis

MH Construction, Inc. follows all relevant OSHA safety and health standards. Each job site will have specific OSHA guidelines and regulations that must always be adhered to while on site, and the Superintendent of the site will conduct a daily Job Hazard Analysis (Tailgate) for the scope of work to be done that day. Team members are expected to attend these briefings, wear all required personal protective equipment, and follow the site-specific safety plan at all times. A related acknowledgment form (Employee Safety Policy Acknowledgement) accompanies this handbook.

Reporting Injuries and Incidents

If you are injured or become ill on the job, you must immediately report it to your manager and the Human Resources Department so the company can help you obtain appropriate medical treatment. Prompt reporting protects your health, supports an accurate investigation, and ensures your workers' compensation benefits are not delayed or jeopardized. Near-misses and unsafe conditions should also be reported promptly so that hazards can be corrected before an injury occurs. Details of workers' compensation coverage are provided in the Compensation & Benefits chapter.

Workplace Security and Non-Violence

MH Construction, Inc. is committed to maintaining a workplace that is free of violence. The safety and security of our team members is of the utmost importance. Acts of violence or threats of violence—whether verbal, physical, or implied—will not be tolerated and will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination. Team members should immediately report any threat, act of violence, or suspicious activity to their supervisor, the General Manager, or the President so it can be addressed quickly.

Substance-Free Worksites

A safe worksite depends on every team member being fit for duty. Working under the influence of alcohol or controlled substances endangers everyone on site. The company's full expectations, including screening requirements, are detailed in the Code of Conduct chapter and apply on all job sites and company property.

Inclement Weather

MH Construction, Inc. functions best when all team members are at work, so it is important to report to work as scheduled. If hazardous weather conditions exist, MH Construction, Inc. expects you to use good judgment, and your supervisor will take that into consideration if you do not arrive on time. Your safety while traveling to and from a job site is never worth an unnecessary risk.
