Holidays

MH Construction, Inc. recognizes the following holidays as paid holidays for all team members:

  • New Year's Day
  • Memorial Day
  • Independence Day
  • Labor Day
  • Veterans Day
  • Thanksgiving Day
  • Day after Thanksgiving
  • Christmas Day
  • Floating Holiday

Should a holiday fall on a Saturday, the preceding Friday will be considered the paid holiday; if a holiday falls on a Sunday, the following Monday will be considered the paid holiday. Team members who work in a department that regularly operates seven days a week will be scheduled for an alternate day off if they work on the holiday. Team members who do not perform any work during the week in which a paid holiday occurs, and who are not using approved PTO or sick time during that week, are not eligible to receive holiday pay. If your job requires you to work on one of these holidays, you may be paid overtime. If a paid holiday occurs during your vacation, you will not be charged for a vacation day, and you may extend your vacation by an additional day if you choose.

Personal Time and Paid Time Off (PTO)

Personal time is any time a team member is not at work during their normal work schedule and may be used for sickness, vacation, compassionate leave, maternity leave, or any other reason. Arrangements for personal time off must be made with the supervisor as far in advance as possible; at least two weeks' notice is required for scheduling more than two days of personal time off, and all personal time off must be made on a request form provided and approved by your supervisor. A physician's note is required for team members missing three or more days of work due to illness. If conflicts arise due to overlapping requests, management will decide on a case-by-case basis; a reasonable effort will be made to grant time off, though departmental workload may limit extended personal time off at certain times of the year.

Hourly and salaried team members are eligible for PTO. Personal time is allocated according to length of service and date of hire as follows:

Length of Service PTO Allocation
At date of hire 40 hours PTO
First of the following calendar year after hire date 80 hours PTO
First of the second calendar year after hire date 80 hours PTO

PTO is separate from sick time accrual, and no PTO will carry forward to the next year—PTO allocation resets January 1st of each year unless authorized in writing by management. For hourly team members, when personal time is used up and you are not at work, you will not be paid for the time missed. For salaried team members, when personal time is used up and you are not at work for a full day, or if no work is performed for an entire work week, you will not be paid for the time missed. PTO does not count as hours worked and does not count toward overtime.

Bereavement Leave

MH Construction, Inc. understands that the loss of a loved one is a profoundly distressing experience. Our bereavement policy supports team members during this difficult time by providing necessary time off to grieve, attend funeral services, and manage related affairs. All full-time team members who have completed their probationary period are eligible for paid bereavement leave. A dependent—an immediate family member such as a spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, or sibling—entitles a team member to five working days of leave, and five working days are provided for the death of a child. For a non-dependent extended family member (such as a cousin, aunt, uncle, niece, or nephew) or a close friend, discretionary leave of up to five days may be taken. Recognizing the emotional impact of a miscarriage, the company provides five working days of special leave for such circumstances.

Team members should inform their direct supervisor of their need for bereavement leave as soon as possible; documentation such as a death certificate may be requested to process the leave. Team members may use their annual leave entitlements in conjunction with bereavement leave, and unpaid leave may be available for extended periods subject to managerial approval. MH Construction, Inc. supports a phased return to work, considering individual readiness and capacity to resume work, and the impact of bereavement on concentration and wellbeing will be considered in health and safety assessments, especially for roles requiring high levels of attention or operating machinery. The company recognizes and respects diverse cultural practices and beliefs surrounding death and grief, and team members are encouraged to communicate any specific needs or observances required during their time of bereavement.

Sick Leave

All team members of MH Construction, Inc. are entitled to sick leave pay. Sick leave accrues from the first day of employment at a rate of one hour per 40 hours worked and is available to use after 90 days of employment. Sick leave may be used for a team member's own mental or physical illness, injury, or health condition (including preventive care); to care for a family member with such a condition; when the team member's workplace or a child's school or place of care has been closed by order of a public official for a health-related reason; and for absences that qualify under the state's Domestic Violence Leave Act (DVLA). Covered family members include a child, parent, spouse, registered domestic partner, grandparent, or grandchild, as defined by applicable law.

Any team member needing to use more than three consecutive days of sick leave shall provide physician verification that the purpose meets the policy. Sick leave that occurs immediately before or after requested time off shall also require physician verification. Proper notice is required, whether sick or not, for unscheduled absences so the time off does not become an unexcused absence; you must notify your manager prior to the start of work if you will be absent. Scheduled time away, such as doctor's appointments, must be properly arranged with your manager, with notice of when you are leaving and returning so that accurate sick leave time can be recorded.

Leave of Absence

A leave of absence is an approved period of inactive employment. Although part or all of an approved leave of absence may be unpaid, your benefits will continue in effect during this period provided you pay all required premiums (for the full amount of the premium) on a timely basis. If your absence is subject to the Family Medical Leave Act, the employer portion of your benefit premiums will continue to be paid by MH Construction, Inc. for up to 12 weeks, and you will be responsible for the team-member portion of the premium owed. Maternity leave is considered a leave of absence, except that a team member may first use all accumulated personal time prior to any leave of absence. MH Construction, Inc. supports your right to vote and urges you to do so; salaried team members will be allowed up to one hour off with pay for voting in state and federal elections, provided you get your supervisor's approval in advance. Team members required to be absent for military duty will be accorded all benefits provided by law and must provide military orders related to the expected absence.

Travel and Reimbursement

If team members are required to use their own vehicle for company business, they will be reimbursed for their fuel; this does not include daily travel to the job site. Related parking expenses for a company-approved trip will be reimbursed with presentation of a receipt, and receipts should be turned in during the pay period in which they occur. Traffic and parking violations will not be reimbursed. Expenses other than in-town job-related travel by staff members must be pre-approved. If you must entertain prospective Client Partners or others while on a trip, keep the expenses reasonable; you will be reimbursed for reasonable expenses that are pre-approved by the President.

If you use your personal vehicle for a company errand or trip, you must be prepared to take all legal or civil responsibility for your driving and ensure your vehicle is in safe driving condition. The company will not be responsible for any accident, theft, or other occurrence when you are using your own vehicle. The fuel reimbursement you receive includes wear and tear as well as insurance for the miles you use your car. You may not use your vehicle for a company errand or trip if you do not have insurance to cover any potential accident, theft, or other vehicle-related occurrence, and by using your own vehicle you agree to indemnify the company from any liability due to your own driving.
