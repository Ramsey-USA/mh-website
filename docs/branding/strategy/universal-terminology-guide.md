## 🎯 Purpose

This guide defines **universal language and terminology standards** that should be used consistently across all MH
Construction communications—website, marketing materials, proposals, emails, and verbal communications. These
standards ensure brand coherence and professional consistency company-wide.

### Core Principle

**Consistency builds trust.** When we use the same terms the same way across all touchpoints, we strengthen our
brand identity and make our message clearer to clients, partners, and team members.

---

## 📖 TERMINOLOGY CATEGORIES

### 1. PARTNERSHIP LANGUAGE

#### **Client Partners** (PREFERRED - UNIVERSAL STANDARD)

**Definition:** Businesses, organizations, or individuals who hire MH Construction for project work. They are the
project owners/decision-makers.

**When to Use:**

- Referring to current or past clients
- Describing client relationships
- Marketing to potential clients
- Throughout all website pages
- In proposals and communications

**Approved Variations:**

- "Client Partner" (singular - PREFERRED)
- "Client Partners" (plural)
- "our Client Partners"
- "Client Partner relationships"

**Examples of Correct Usage:**

```text
✅ "What Our Client Partners Say"
✅ "Building trust with every Client Partner"
✅ "Client Partner success is our success"
✅ "We work WITH our Client Partners"
✅ "Client Partner Testimonials"
```

**FORBIDDEN Terms (DO NOT USE):**

- ❌ "clients" (too transactional, lacks relationship emphasis)
- ❌ "customers" (retail language, not appropriate for B2B construction)
- ❌ "partners" alone (ambiguous - could mean trade partners or clients)
- ❌ "clientele" (too formal/pretentious)
- ❌ "patrons" (incorrect industry usage)

---

#### **Trade Partners** (DISTINCT FROM CLIENT PARTNERS)

**Definition:** Subcontractors, vendors, suppliers, and industry professionals we collaborate with on projects.
They are NOT the client.

**When to Use:**

- Referring to subcontractors/vendors
- Trade Partners page specifically
- Describing supply chain relationships
- In contractor network communications

**Approved Variations:**

- "Trade Partner" (singular)
- "Trade Partners" (plural)
- "our Trade Partners"
- "trusted Trade Partners"
- "Trade Partner network"

**Examples of Correct Usage:**

```text
✅ "Join our Trade Partner network"
✅ "We treat Trade Partners with respect"
✅ "Certified Trade Partners"
✅ "Building relationships with Trade Partners"
```

**CRITICAL DISTINCTION:**

- **Client Partners** = Those who HIRE us (project owners)
- **Trade Partners** = Those we HIRE or work alongside (subcontractors/vendors)

**Never interchange these terms.** Using "Trade Partners" when referring to clients, or "Client Partners" when
referring to subcontractors creates confusion.

### 2. VETERAN & MILITARY LANGUAGE

#### **Veteran Status (CONSISTENT STANDARD)**

**PREFERRED Standard Phrasing:**

- "Veteran-Owned" (capitalized, hyphenated)
- "Army veteran leadership" (lowercase "veteran" when describing individual)
- "veteran-owned since January 2025"
- "veteran-fueled reliability"

**Approved Variations:**

- "Veteran-Owned Excellence"
- "Veteran-Owned & Operated"
- "veteran-owned team"
- "veteran-owned business"
- "veteran-owned values"

**Examples of Correct Usage:**

```text
✅ "Founded 2010, Veteran-Owned since 2025"
✅ "Army veteran leadership brings military precision"
✅ "Our veteran-owned team understands discipline"
✅ "Purchased by Army veteran Jeremy Thamert in January 2025"
```

**Consistency Rules:**

- Capitalize "Veteran-Owned" when used as proper descriptor
- Use lowercase "veteran" when describing individuals ("Army veteran," "combat veteran")
- Always hyphenate "veteran-owned"
- Always include "since January 2025" when stating veteran ownership status

**FORBIDDEN:**

- ❌ "Vet-owned" (too casual)
- ❌ "Veteran owned" (missing hyphen)
- ❌ "veteran owned" (wrong capitalization when used as proper descriptor)
- ❌ "Military-owned" (inaccurate - veterans aren't active military)

---

#### **Military Precision Language**

**PREFERRED Phrases:**

- "military precision"
- "military discipline"
- "tactical briefing/assessment/planning"
- "mission-focused"
- "mission-ready"

**Examples of Correct Usage:**

```text
✅ "Award-winning team bringing military precision to construction"
✅ "Mission-focused project execution"
✅ "Military discipline meets construction excellence"
✅ "Veteran leadership brings proven expertise"
```

**Guidelines:**

- Use military terminology respectfully and appropriately
- Don't over-militarize everyday construction terms
- Balance military precision language with partnership warmth

**AVOID:**

- ❌ Over-using military jargon that clients won't understand
- ❌ Making every construction term sound military ("deploy" for "deliver," etc.)
- ❌ Trivializing military service with casual references

### 3. LOCATION & GEOGRAPHIC TERMS

#### **Service Areas (STANDARD ORDER)**

**Primary Standard Format:**

```text
Licensed in WA, OR, ID
OR
Licensed in Washington, Oregon, Idaho
```

**Always list in this order:** Washington → Oregon → Idaho

**Headquarters:**

```text
3111 N. Capitol Ave.
Pasco, WA 99301
```

**Regional References:**

- "Pacific Northwest" (PREFERRED for regional identity)
- "Tri-Cities" (when referring to local area)
- "Tri-Cities Area" or "Tri-Cities Region"
- "Pasco, Kennewick, Richland" (full listing when needed)
- "Eastern Washington region"
- "Pacific Northwest communities"

**Examples of Correct Usage:**

```text
✅ "Serving the Pacific Northwest"
✅ "Licensed in WA, OR, ID"
✅ "Headquartered in Pasco, WA"
✅ "Tri-Cities construction excellence"
✅ "Pacific Northwest premium materials"
```

**Consistency Rules:**

- Always use "Pacific Northwest" (not "PNW" in formal content)
- When listing states, use abbreviations OR full names consistently within same content block
- Tri-Cities = Pasco, Kennewick, Richland (this specific area)

### 6. SAFETY LANGUAGE

#### **Safety Record (EXACT STANDARDS)**

**Official Safety Record Statements:**

```text
✅ "0.6 EMR safety record"
✅ ".6 EMR award-winning safety record"
✅ "Industry-leading 0.6 EMR (40% better than industry average)"
✅ "Award-Winning Safety Record"
✅ "Multiple AGC-WA Top EMR Awards"
✅ "OSHA VPP Star designation"
✅ "3+ years without time-loss injuries"
✅ "3+ consecutive years without time-loss injuries"
```

**Always Include Full Context When Stating EMR:**

- State the 0.6 number
- Mention it's 40% better than industry average (1.0)
- Reference AGC-WA awards when appropriate

**Examples of Correct Usage:**

```text
✅ "Our .6 EMR is 40% better than industry average"
✅ "Award-winning safety with .6 EMR safety record"
✅ "Industry-leading 0.6 EMR safety record"
✅ "OSHA VPP Star designation demonstrates commitment"
```

**Safety Certifications:**

- "OSHA 30-Hour Certified" (team certification)
- "OSHA VPP Star designation" (company achievement)
- "AGC-WA Top EMR Awards"

**Consistency Rules:**

- Use "0.6 EMR" or ".6 EMR" (both acceptable, be consistent within same content)
- Always lowercase "industry average"
- VPP = Voluntary Protection Program
- Include context (40% better) when space allows

## 🚫 COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### 1. Partnership Terminology Errors

```text
❌ "Our clients appreciate our work"
✅ "Our Client Partners appreciate our collaborative approach"

❌ "We work for you"
✅ "We work WITH you"

❌ "Our partners" (ambiguous)
✅ "Our Client Partners" OR "Our Trade Partners" (specific)
```

### 2. Location Inconsistencies

```text
❌ "Licensed in Idaho, Oregon, Washington" (wrong order)
✅ "Licensed in WA, OR, ID" OR "Licensed in Washington, Oregon, Idaho"

❌ "Serving the PNW"
✅ "Serving the Pacific Northwest"
```

### 3. Safety Record Errors

```text
❌ "0.6 EMR" (without context)
✅ "0.6 EMR - 40% better than industry average"

❌ "Best safety record in the industry"
✅ "Award-winning .6 EMR safety record with multiple AGC-WA Top EMR Awards"
```

### 4. Technology Balance Errors

```text
❌ "Fully automated project management" (suggests no human involvement)
✅ "Digital tools support our hands-on project management"

❌ "No need for meetings - we handle it all online"
✅ "Online resources complement our face-to-face consultation"
```

### 5. Slogan Misuse

```text
❌ "THE ROI IS THE RELATIONSHIP" on homepage (wrong context)
✓ "Building projects for the client, NOT the dollar" on homepage

❌ "Building for the Trade Partner" (never alter the slogan)
✓ "Building projects for the client, NOT the dollar" (unchanged, with Trade Partner context explained separately)
```
