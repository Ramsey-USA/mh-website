## üìã USAGE GUIDELINES

### When to Use Military Terminology

‚úÖ **APPROPRIATE CONTEXTS:**

1. **Veteran Foundation Pages** (Home, About, Veterans)
   - Heavy military-construction integration
   - Emphasizes service-earned values
   - Showcases veteran leadership

2. **Professional Services Pages** (Services, Projects, Government)
   - Moderate military-construction integration
   - Focus on proven expertise
   - Highlight discipline and thoroughness

3. **Partnership Pages** (Team, Careers, Trade Partners)
   - Balanced military-construction terminology
   - Emphasizes values and reliability
   - Showcases service-earned character

4. **Communication Pages** (Contact, FAQ, Urgent)
   - Light military-construction integration
   - Clear, direct communication
   - Professional military bearing

### Professional Integration Principles

1. **Natural Flow**: Military terms should read naturally in context
2. **Audience Awareness**: Balance veteran authenticity with civilian accessibility
3. **Consistency**: Use the same term consistently throughout the site
4. **Clarify When Needed**: Add brief explanations for less common terms
5. **Avoid Overuse**: Don't force military terms where standard language works better

### Examples of Professional Integration

#### ‚úÖ EXCELLENT - Natural & Professional

> "Schedule your initial project mission brief to discuss your construction objectives and receive a comprehensive tactical plan with transparent pricing."

> "We provide weekly project SITREPs (situation reports) so you're never in the dark about timeline, budget, or progress."

> "From pre-construction recon to final after-action review, military precision guides every phase of your build."

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è AVOID - Forced or Excessive

> "Our tactical construction operators will execute precision building operations within your AO according to mission ROE and deliver regular SITREP intel for your mission command review."

## üö´ TERMS TO AVOID

### Avoid Overly Technical Military Jargon

‚ùå **TOO TECHNICAL:**

- DEFCON, OPSEC, FUBAR, SNAFU
- MOS, PT, TAC, BDU
- Oscar Mike, WILCO, Lima Charlie
- Hard copy, weapons hot, etc.

### Avoid Combat-Specific Terminology

‚ùå **TOO AGGRESSIVE:**

- "Assault the project"
- "Target the deadline"
- "Weapon system" (for equipment)
- "Engage the enemy" (for problems)
- "Kill zone" or "hot zone"

### Avoid Exploitation Language

‚ùå **EXPLOITATIVE:**

- Using military service as sales pressure
- Implying military superiority over civilians
- Trading on sacrifice for business gain
- Over-romanticizing military service

## üéØ QUICK REFERENCE CHECKLIST

Before using military-construction terminology, ask:

- [ ] Does it sound natural in context?
- [ ] Will both veteran and civilian audiences understand it?
- [ ] Does it add authentic value or just sound "military"?
- [ ] Am I being respectful of military service?
- [ ] Is it consistent with other usage on the site?
- [ ] Does it enhance or distract from the message?

**If you answer "no" to any question, reconsider the usage.**
