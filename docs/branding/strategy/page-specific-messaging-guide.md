## üéØ Purpose

This guide defines the **unique voice, tone, and messaging strategy** for each page group on the
MH Construction website. While our core values remain constant, each page group has its own
personality and communication style designed to resonate with its specific audience.

### Core Principle

**"Building for the Client, NOT the Dollar"** is our PRIMARY SLOGAN and should be prominently
featured across the site. However, each page group also has freedom to create and use
supplementary messaging that speaks to its unique purpose.

## üèõÔ∏è GROUP 1: Traditional Business Foundation

### Pages: Home, Contact, Booking

### Voice & Tone

- **Authentic:** Real people, real handshakes, real relationships
- **Timeless:** Old-school values that never go out of style
- **Trustworthy:** Your word is your bond mentality
- **Personal:** Face-to-face, human connection emphasized

### Primary Messaging

**Main Slogan (Featured Prominently):**

- "Building for the Client, NOT the Dollar"

**Supporting Slogans:**

- "THE ROI IS THE RELATIONSHIP"
- "Where Handshakes Still Matter"
- "Your Word Is Your Bond - So Is Ours"

### Language Characteristics

- Use traditional business terminology
- Emphasize face-to-face meetings and personal connection
- Reference timeless values: integrity, handshake deals, keeping your word
- Contrast modern world with old-school principles
- Warm, welcoming, approachable

### Key Phrases

- "Where handshakes matter and your word is your bond"
- "Traditional business values in a modern world"
- "Face-to-face consultation where trust begins"
- "Founded on integrity, built on relationships"
- "The way business should be done"

### Avoid

- Overly corporate language
- Tech jargon (save for tech pages)
- Impersonal automation messaging
- Sales pressure tactics

## üìö GROUP 2: Company Heritage

### Pages: About, Projects

### Voice & Tone

- **Proud:** Celebrating achievements and milestones
- **Reflective:** Looking back at the journey
- **Credible:** Evidence-based, proven track record
- **Narrative:** Storytelling approach to company history

### Primary Messaging

**Main Slogan:**

- "Building for the Client, NOT the Dollar" (as foundation)

**Heritage-Specific Slogans:**

- "Trust Built, Project by Project"
- "Excellence Through Experience"
- "Where Precision Meets Partnership"
- "150+ Years of Combined Excellence"

### Language Characteristics

- Use past-tense achievements as proof points
- Tell the company's story chronologically
- Highlight transformative moments and growth
- Connect past success to present capabilities
- Reference founding principles and how they endure

### Key Phrases

- "Founded in 2010, veteran-owned since January 2025"
- "Decades of experience brought together"
- "Built on a foundation of trust"
- "Our history speaks for itself"
- "From humble beginnings to regional leadership"
- "Every project tells a story"

### Avoid

- Future promises without past proof
- Generic industry claims
- Downplaying company age or evolution
- Forgetting to connect heritage to current values

## üá∫üá∏ GROUP 4: Professional & Patriotic

### Pages: Government, Veterans

### Voice & Tone

- **Respectful:** Honoring service and sacrifice
- **Professional:** Meeting federal standards and expectations
- **Mission-focused:** Commitment to duty and excellence
- **Patriotic:** Proud American values without being over-the-top

### Primary Messaging

**Main Slogan:**

- "Building for the Client, NOT the Dollar" (as foundation)

- "Mission-Ready, Compliance-Driven"
- "Veteran Values, Community-Focused Results"
- "Serving Those Who Served"
- "Excellence in Every Detail"

### Language Characteristics

- Use military-adjacent terminology appropriately
- Reference veteran values: duty, integrity, mission focus
- Emphasize regulatory compliance and federal standards
- Show respect for government processes
- Honor veteran sacrifice without exploitation

### Key Phrases

- "Army veteran leadership since January 2025"
- "Military precision meets construction excellence"
- "Serving those who served our nation"
- "Mission-focused project execution"
- "Compliance-driven, quality-assured"
- "Your word is your bond - we understand that"

- Over-militarization of language
- Exploiting veteran status for gain
- Casual references to military service
- Assuming all clients understand military jargon
- Political messaging or partisanship

## üíª GROUP 6: Tech Innovation

### Pages: AI Estimator, 3D Explorer

### Voice & Tone

- **Tech-savvy:** Modern, innovative, forward-thinking
- **Educational:** Explaining technology accessibly
- **Confident:** Comfortable with innovation
- **Balanced:** Tech as tool, not replacement for relationship

### Primary Messaging

**Main Slogan:**

- "Building for the Client, NOT the Dollar" (still applies)

**Tech-Focused Slogans:**

- "Smart Planning Starts Here"
- "Innovation Meets Construction Excellence"
- "Technology Serving Traditional Values"
- "AI-Powered, Human-Centered"

### Language Characteristics

- Use tech terminology, but explain it
- Emphasize technology as enhancement, not replacement
- Show how innovation serves client needs
- Balance cutting-edge with proven methods
- Maintain warmth despite technical subject

### Key Phrases

- "AI-powered estimation meets personal consultation"
- "Technology that serves, never replaces, relationships"
- "Smart tools for smarter planning"
- "Innovation with integrity"
- "Where data meets decades of experience"
- "Cutting-edge tools, old-school values"

### Avoid

- Tech jargon without explanation
- Implying automation replaces people
- Cold, impersonal technology language
- Over-hyping capabilities
- Forgetting the human element

### Pages: Trade Partners, Urgent Support

- **Partnership-oriented:** Mutual success language

- Use business-to-business language
- Show responsiveness and availability
- Highlight partnership value clearly
- Quantify benefits when possible

### Key Phrases (Trade Partners)

- "THE ROI IS THE RELATIONSHIP - and we prove it"
- "Partnerships that profit everyone"
- "Fair payment, clear communication, mutual success"
- "Building your business while building ours"
- "Professional partnerships, profitable results"

### Key Phrases (Urgent Support)

- "When time is critical, we respond"
- "24/7 emergency support for your peace of mind"

- "THE ROI IS THE RELATIONSHIP - even in emergencies"
- "Your urgent need is our immediate priority"

### Avoid

- Transactional language without relationship emphasis
- Vague partnership promises
- Unclear ROI claims
- Slow/unresponsive tone
- Ignoring business realities

## ‚úÖ Implementation Guidelines

### For Content Writers

1. **Identify the page group** before writing any content
2. **Lead with the appropriate voice and tone** for that group
3. **Use "Building for the Client, NOT the Dollar"** as the primary slogan across all pages
4. **Integrate group-specific slogans** as secondary messaging
5. **Maintain consistency** within each page group
6. **Test messaging** against the characteristics for that group

### For Developers

1. **Reference this guide** when implementing page content
2. **Ensure hero sections** reflect appropriate voice
3. **Use correct slogan hierarchy** (primary vs. secondary)
4. **Maintain group-specific tone** in all content blocks
5. **Review page content** against this guide before deployment

### For Marketing Team

1. **Align campaigns** with page-specific messaging
2. **Use appropriate voice** for different audience segments
3. **Maintain brand consistency** while allowing group flexibility
4. **Test messaging effectiveness** by group
5. **Update guide** based on market response
